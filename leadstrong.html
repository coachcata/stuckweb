Six signs your brain has developed a stuck pattern.

One of these probably sounds like the room you've been sitting in for months.

i.

Reactive leadership

Firefighting instead of leading. The amygdala's overwhelming the prefrontal cortex, locking you in threat response.

ii.

Going blank

The mind goes blank in high-stakes moments. Stress hormones are shutting down working memory and verbal fluency.

iii.

Running perfectionism

"I should be able to handle this." Chronic amygdala activation around mistakes is creating ongoing threat sensitivity.

iv.

Poor decisions under pressure

When stakes rise, you default to impulsive or overly cautious choices. The amygdala is hijacking control from the PFC.

v.

Stuck in performance mode

Still delivering — but it's taking more than it used to. The brain's in energy-conservation mode, degrading decisions before you notice.

vi.

Capped at your stress tolerance

05 · The Patterns

Six brain patterns we retrain.

Each engagement begins by identifying which one is yours. The protocol is built around it.

i. Reactive Leadership

Amygdala in chronic threat mode

PFC can't access strategic thinking. You're firefighting, stuck in execution, can't think big-picture.

How we retrain

Amygdala downregulation. PFC strengthening through strategic decompression. Leadership architecture redesign.

Most leaders notice the shift by week four.

ii. Going Blank

Stress hormones shutting down working memory

Mind goes blank in high-stakes moments. You lose access to what you know when it matters most.

How we retrain

Stress-response reconditioning. Working memory restoration. Real-time intervention. Progressive stress exposure.

Noticeable by week three. Significant shift by week eight.

iii. Perfectionism

Hyperactive fear-of-failure circuits

"I should be able to handle this." Second-guessing. Shame about struggling. Chronic threat sensitivity.

How we retrain

Threat-response recalibration around evaluation. Separating identity from performance. New feedback loops.

Identity shift by week six. Behavioural change by week ten.

iv. Performance Mode

Chronically depleted prefrontal resources

Running on autopilot. Decisions feel heavy. Functional but not sharp. Energy-conservation mode.

How we retrain

Cognitive capacity restoration through rest protocols. Realignment to operate from strengths. Sustainable rhythms.

Energy shift by week two. Decision quality by week six.

v. Burnout

Structural brain changes from sustained stress

Running on empty. Can't slow down. Not just tired — chronic stress has changed your brain's structure.

How we retrain

Amygdala regulation and PFC restoration. Structured rest as the mechanism for neuroplasticity. Tolerance rebuilt from depletion.

Physical shift by week two. Cognitive restoration by week eight.

vi. Decisions Under Pressure

Threat system overriding strategic evaluation

Impulsive or overly cautious choices when stakes are high. Amygdala hijacking control.

How we retrain

Decision-making circuit rewiring under controlled pressure. Pattern interruption. PFC resilience in high-stakes simulations.

Quality lift by week five. Consistent new pattern by week ten.
