02 · What Teams Carry

Six things every team carries — until they're named.

The org chart can't see these. The strategy deck doesn't help. But everyone in the room feels them.

i.

People searching for their purpose

Within the whole. Each individual quietly asking where they actually fit.

ii.

Alignment that never quite sticks

Direction is set in the room and lost by Friday. The work pulls people apart again.

iii.

One-to-one conflicts draining energy

Unspoken tension between two people, slowly contaminating the whole team's atmosphere.

iv.

Historic issues nobody has named

Things that happened months or years ago. Still felt. Still shaping how the team behaves.

v.

Silos hardening as people retreat

Collaboration thins. People withdraw into their own remit. The team stops thinking together.

vi.

The fun has gone

And with it, engagement. Work has become heavy. The energy that built the team is fading.

Plate i · The architecture
03 · The Method

Reconnect. Then sustain.

Two phases across twelve weeks. First we build the foundation in person. Then we hold the change in place until the team carries it themselves.

Phase i.Weeks 1 – 4 Reconnection & synergy. Individual CliftonStrengths assessment and 1:1 coaching with every team member in weeks 1–3. Then in week 4, the team comes together for a full Experience Day in London.
Phase ii.Weeks 5 – 12 Sustained connection. Four bi-weekly group coaching sessions that respond to what's actually surfacing in the team — not a pre-set curriculum. Plus 1:1 check-ins between each session. By week 12, the team holds the space themselves.
Throughout The Leader Side Quest. Separate 1:1 sessions for the team leader running parallel to the programme — how to lead through what the work surfaces, and hold the new culture once it lands.

05 · The Experience Day

One day. Three rooms. The team comes back different.

Week four. A full day at The O2 in London — venue, food, and activities all included in the programme.

i. Morning · The work
Morning

The workshop

On-site at The O2

The team maps strengths, names the real friction points, and builds a shared operating system. This is where the serious work happens — facilitated, structured, and pointed at what's actually been getting in the way.

ii. Lunch · The table
Midday

Breaking bread together

TOCA Social, The O2

A proper meal. The conversation that started in the workshop carries into a shared table — the way real teams have always bonded. Not networking. Not performance. Just eating, talking, becoming people to each other again.

iii. Afternoon · The play
Afternoon

Playing together

Activate at The O2

The world's first interactive gaming facility. The team plays through nine dynamic game rooms in cooperative mode — physical, playful, built for groups. Laughter, movement, shared experience. The bonding the morning made possible.

By the end of the day, the team has done serious work together and had serious fun together. That combination is what sets the foundation for the next eight weeks.

06 · The Theme

Six themes the work can be built around.

Every TeamStrong is built around one central theme — explored in 1:1s, anchored on the Experience Day, embedded in group coaching. Choose one, or agree something different on the call.

i. From fearing AI to leading with it Help the team stop worrying about AI and start using it to do better, higher-value work — together. Best forTeams facing transformation
ii. New leader integration A new leader has joined. Help everyone get to know each other properly — who this person is, what they're about, how to work together from day one. Best forTeams with new leadership
iii. Raising the standard Good enough isn't good enough any more. For teams ready to raise their own bar and hold each other to it. Best forTeams ready to level up
iv. Embracing innovation Stop doing things the way they've always been done. Build a team that looks for better, not just familiar. Best forTeams stuck in defaults
v. Stepping out of fear to take risks Play it safe and nothing changes. Help the team get comfortable with discomfort and back themselves. Best forRisk-averse teams
vi. Strategic thinking as a team Stop working in silos. Start thinking together about the bigger picture — where the team fits and what decisions need making now. Best forTeams in silos
