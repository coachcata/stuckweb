The Problem

You've Been Promoted.
Now What?

Every senior leader hits the same wall. The skills that got you here — delivery, execution, managing down — are not the skills that get you there. The transition from Director to VP, from VP to C-Suite, demands a fundamentally different way of operating.

Most leaders figure this out after the promotion, when the stakes are already real. They learn on the job, in public, with every misstep visible to the people they're supposed to be leading.

There's a better way. Catalyst exists to close the gap between where you are and where you need to be — before the gap closes on you.

The readiness gap
68% of newly promoted leaders say they weren't prepared for the demands of their new role.
The silent cost
Regrettable attrition at senior level costs 3–5x salary. Most of it is preventable.
The competitor risk
Your best people are always being courted. The ones who feel underdeveloped leave first.
The Method

Three Phases.
One Outcome.

Every engagement follows the same arc: see clearly, practise deliberately, operate differently. The depth and duration depend on where you are and where you need to be.

01

See

Understand how you're actually operating — not how you think you're operating. Map the gap between your current level and the level above. Identify the specific behaviours, blind spots and defaults that are holding the transition back.

02

Practise

Build the capability in private. Work through real scenarios from the level above — the board conversation, the strategic trade-off, the stakeholder conflict — before you face them live. Deliberate practice, not theory.

03

Operate

Shift from practising to performing. Apply the new capability in real situations, with coaching support to consolidate the shift. The goal is not insight — it's changed behaviour that sticks.
