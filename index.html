02 · The Gap

Where the thinking time goes.

Meetings aren't designed for thinking. They're designed for alignment and information transfer.

Email isn't designed for thinking. It's designed for response.

Team updates aren't designed for thinking. They're designed for visibility.

The board isn't designed for thinking. It's designed for governance.

Even the executive assistant is optimising the leader's time away from thinking, not toward it. Toward more meetings. More replies. More board prep.

The Whiteboard Sessions are the hour in the week that is.

03 · The Work

The work moves at three altitudes.

The session takes the altitude your problem needs.

i.

Strategic

The next eighteen months. The board narrative. The shape of growth.

ii.

Mid-altitude

Restructures and hiring. The change you need to land. The conversation you've been avoiding.

iii.

Tactical

The meeting on Tuesday. The email you keep rewriting. The call you've been ducking.

04 · The Session

One whiteboard. Your hardest problem. Two people thinking.

The session externalises what you've been carrying, pressure-tests it live, and ends in a conclusion you can act on by Monday.

i. ExternaliseGet what's swirling in your head onto the board.
ii. Pressure-testTalk through the options out loud, live.
iii. ConcludeLeave with a decision, not a homework prompt.
