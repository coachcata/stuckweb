02 · Who It's For

Four signs the brain has gone past tired into depleted.

Each describes a structural state in the brain — not a mood, not a phase. And each responds to the same protocol.

i.

Stuck in decision fog

Decisions that used to be easy now feel impossible. The prefrontal cortex is depleted and the amygdala is in control.

ii.

Stuck in cognitive depletion

The brain has slipped into energy-conservation mode. Still functional, but no longer sharp. Sustained cognitive load has degraded PFC efficiency.

iii.

Stuck in burnout at the brain level

Chronic stress has changed the brain's structure. Enlarged amygdala. Impaired frontal regions. Degraded decision-making capacity. And you can't slow down.

iv.

Preparing for high-stakes pressure

Board presentation. Fundraising roadshow. Merger negotiation. Stress tolerance needs to be raised before the pressure peaks — not during.

56% of executives experience chronic cognitive overload. Decision fatigue costs them 40% of productive thinking time.

Most leaders don't recognise depletion until performance visibly drops. By then, they've been operating below capacity for months.

03 · The Protocol

One day. Three movements. Pause. Reset. Go.

Each retreat day follows the same evidence-based architecture. This is not time off. It is a performance restoration protocol.

i. Morning · Pause
Morning

Physical reset

A guided movement session releases physical tension and shifts the nervous system out of chronic stress mode. Includes blindfolded movement protocols — removing vision forces attention inward to bodily sensations.

Amygdala downregulation through somatic practice. The body holds cognitive tension. Moving with intention shifts the brain from reactive to receptive.

ii. Midday · Reset
Midday

Strategic clarity

Private one-on-one coaching to remove cognitive noise and restore perspective. White-boarding to expand thinking capability. Powerful questions that strip away noise and reconnect you to what actually matters.

Clarity does not come from thinking harder. It comes from thinking differently while the prefrontal cortex is temporarily back online.

iii. Afternoon · Go
Afternoon

Integration & restoration

Exclusive access to the spa and a 60-minute treatment. Not passive rest. Structured restoration. The brain processes and embeds new thinking during rest, not during active work. The spa creates conditions for neuroplasticity.

This is where the clarity becomes lasting change. Not just a temporary shift.
