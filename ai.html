The Four Patterns That Keep
Teams Stuck on AI

Most teams aren't anti-AI. They're stuck in one of these patterns — and none of them get solved by sending a link to ChatGPT.

The Pretenders

They say they're using AI. In reality, a couple of people have tried it and the rest are waiting to see what happens. Nobody is using it in a way that changes how the team works.

The Paralysed

They know it matters but they're afraid — of looking stupid, of making mistakes, of being replaced. So they do nothing and hope someone else figures it out first.

Individuals are experimenting in isolation. There's no shared approach, no team strategy, no connection between what people are trying and what the business actually needs.

The Resistant

A handful of people have decided AI isn't for them. They're vocal about it. And their resistance is slowing the whole team down — whether they realise it or not.

The programme

How TeamStrong Solves This

This isn't a training day. It's a 12-week coaching programme that rewires how your team thinks about, talks about, and uses AI together.

Weeks 1–3

Individual Sessions

Each team member works with John 1:1 to understand their personal blueprint — who they are through CliftonStrengths and FIT16, how they fit into the team, what they contribute, and specifically how AI can enhance the value they bring to their role and the wider organisation.

Week 4

Experience Day at The O2

The team comes together at The O2 for a full day off-site. The focus: how do we use AI to enhance what we do as a team? The session runs through the morning and picks back up after lunch — mapping processes, spotting waste, and identifying where to redirect energy into higher-value work. Then late afternoon at Activate — competitive, fun, and nothing to do with work.

Weeks 5–12

Sustained Connection

Four bi-weekly group coaching sessions that respond to what's actually happening in the team — not a pre-set curriculum. What surfaces in the room dictates where we go. Between group sessions, each team member has a 1:1 check-in with John — short, focused, and personal. A space to process what's shifting and stay accountable. By Week 12, the team holds the space themselves.

Leader Side Quest — Runs Throughout

Separate 1:1 sessions for the team leader — focused on how to lead a team through AI adoption. Moving them from fear to confidence. Getting the best out of people who are nervous, resistant, or unsure where to start.

Honest answers

What Leaders Usually Ask

We've already done AI training. Why would this be different?

Most AI training teaches tools. This programme starts with people — who they are, how they work, and what's actually blocking them. The AI part only lands when the human part is addressed first. That's why one-off workshops don't stick and this does.

My team isn't technical. Won't this be over their heads?

This isn't a technical programme. Nobody is learning to code. Each person discovers how AI fits into the work they're already doing — based on their actual strengths and role, not a generic syllabus. If they can use email, they can do this.

We can't take the whole team out for 12 weeks.

You're not. Individual sessions are 60 minutes. Group sessions are 90 minutes every two weeks. The Experience Day is one day. The rest happens alongside normal work — and the programme is designed to make the team's actual work better, not to add more to their plate.

Can't we just give everyone a ChatGPT licence?

You can. Most organisations have. The result is usually a handful of enthusiasts, a majority who tried it once and forgot, and no change to how the team actually works together. A tool without a team strategy is just another subscription.

What if some of my team are genuinely resistant to AI?

That's exactly who this is for. Resistance usually comes from fear — fear of looking stupid, fear of being replaced, fear of change. The individual sessions in Weeks 1–3 create a safe space to work through that before anyone has to perform in front of the group.
