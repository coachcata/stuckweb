01 · The Problem
You're senior enough that the hardest decisions land on you. There's no one obvious to think them through with.

Your team is too close. Your peers are competing for the same resources. Your boss has their own agenda.

So you carry it. The board paper. The reorg. The senior hire. The team conversation you've been avoiding for a year.

And the right answer on paper isn't always the right answer in your building — because your building has history, factions, and people who'll quietly resist anything that wasn't their idea.

Where the corporate leader sits

Three lenses I bring to the work.

The Whiteboard Sessions are the practice. These are the lenses I bring to them.

04 · One Thing Worth Knowing
"
Sometimes you're the problem.

Not the situation. Not the team. You.

Most coaching avoids saying this because it's uncomfortable. I'll say it because it's often true — and because the leaders who get the most out of working with me are the ones who want to know when their own pattern is what's keeping them stuck.

If that's not how you want to be worked with, this isn't the right practice for you. If it is, you're in the right place.

— John
