The Problem

Why teams are harder than they look

Every team is a collection of individuals who want to be seen, known, and heard. That alone makes teams complex — because no single leader has the capacity to meet every need in the room.

Add the rest of what teams carry:

  • — People searching for their purpose within the whole
  • — Alignment to direction that's needed but never quite sticks
  • — One-to-one conflicts that quietly drain energy
  • — Historic issues nobody has named but everyone still feels
  • — Silos that harden as people retreat into their own tasks
  • — And somewhere along the way — the fun disappears, and with it, the engagement

This is the real complexity of teams. Not the org chart. Not the strategy deck. The human weight of people trying to work together while carrying all of this at once.

What TeamStrong Does

TeamStrong is a 12-week team coaching programme that addresses all of it — in one intervention.

It helps every person on the team feel genuinely seen, known, and heard. It breaks down the silos that stop collaboration. It surfaces and resolves the one-to-one conflicts that have been quietly corroding trust. It lifts the weight of historic issues. And it brings fun, energy, and connection back into how the team works together.

The Programme

How it works

The programme runs in two phases, grounded in behavioural diagnostics (CliftonStrengths) so the work is built on how each person is actually wired — not guesswork.

Part 1 · Weeks 1–4

Reconnection & Synergy

Individual CliftonStrengths assessment and 1:1 coaching for every team member in the first three weeks.

Then, in Week 4, the team comes together for a full Experience Day at The O2 in London:

  • — Morning workshop (on-site at The O2): the team maps strengths, names the real friction points, and builds a shared operating system
  • — Breaking bread together (lunch): a proper meal at TOCA Social or a similar O2 venue — the conversation that started in the workshop carries into a shared table, the way real teams have always bonded
  • — Afternoon at Activate at The O2: the world's first interactive gaming facility. The team plays together across nine dynamic game rooms — physical, playful, and built for groups. Laughter, movement, and shared experience, in cooperative mode so everyone mingles and works together

By the end of Week 4, the team has done serious work together and had serious fun together. That combination is what sets the foundation for the next eight weeks.

Part 2 · Weeks 5–12

Sustained Connection

Eight weeks of bi-weekly group coaching sessions that embed the new patterns, maintain momentum, resolve emerging friction in real time, and lock in the new way of working together.

Team Workshop Topic of the Month

Choose What Matters Most to Your Team

Every TeamStrong programme is built around a central workshop theme. This is the topic your team explores in individual sessions, brings to life on the Experience Day, and embeds through group coaching. Choose the theme that fits — or agree one during your discovery call.

Most Requested Right Now

From Fearing AI to Leading With It

Help your team stop worrying about AI and start using it to do better work.

Most teams know AI is coming. Few know what to actually do about it. Some are pretending it doesn't apply to them. Others are dabbling but getting nowhere. And the people who could benefit most are often the ones most paralysed by it.

This workshop takes your team from uncertainty to confidence — not by turning them into tech experts, but by helping each person understand how AI enhances the value they already bring.

Weeks 1–3

Individual Sessions

Each team member works with John 1:1 to understand their personal blueprint — who they are through CliftonStrengths and FIT16, how they fit into the team, what they contribute, and specifically how AI can enhance the value they bring to their role and the wider organisation.

Week 4

Experience Day at The O2

Morning through to early afternoon — the team comes together off-site to answer one question: how do we use AI to enhance what we do as a team? This covers process, touchpoints, waste and inefficiencies, and where to redirect energy into higher-value activities that boost the capabilities of the business. Then lunch together. Then an afternoon at Activate — competitive, fun, and nothing to do with work.

Weeks 5–12

Group Coaching

Bi-weekly sessions to embed the shift, hold each other accountable, and make sure the changes actually stick.

Leader Side Quest

Separate 1:1 sessions for the team leader — focused on how to lead a team through AI adoption. Moving them from fear to confidence. Getting the best out of people who are nervous, resistant, or unsure where to start.
